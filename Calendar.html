Groton’s Hidden Treasures Events

Groton’s full program of 11 events found in the Press Release and Discover Hidden Treasures - Groton website. These events include hikes, mountain biking, canoeing/kayaking, geology trolley tours, an introduction to the region’s new fully accessible trail in the forest along the Nashua River, and a presentation on the "Gifts of the Glaciers" that explains the great biodiversity of Groton.

  • Saturday, May 14 - What Glaciers Left Behind – A Geology Trolley Tour
  • Saturday, May 14 - Canoe and Kayak the Nashua River
  • Saturday, May 14 - Rich State Forest (DCR) hike
  • Sunday, May 15 - Rocky Hill (Mass Audubon Sanctuary) hike
  • Sunday, May 15 - Gifts of the Glaciers video
  • Sunday, May 15 - Gibbet Hill hike
  • Wednesday, May 18 - John Tinker Trail walks
  • Wednesday, May 18 - Groton Town Forest mountain bike tour
  • Saturday, May 21 - What Glaciers Left Behind – A Geology Trolley Tour
  • Sunday, May 22 - Gamlin Crystal Spring’s Indian Rock Trail and Boulder Trail hike
  • Sunday, May 22 - Groton Trails mountain bike tour

Groton Trails Committee Meeting

The Groton Trails Committee meeting are held every third Tuesday 7:30 PM at Legion Hall. The meetings are open to the public. Legion Hall is at intersection of School Street, and Hollis Street.
