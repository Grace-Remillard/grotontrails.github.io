Interactive Maps

In addition to the maps included as pdf files on this site, we are pleased to present new trail maps using current GPS and mapping programs.  Groton resident, Chris Marsh, has done a yeoman’s job assembling the trail maps accessible at the links below.  Chris has taken on this endeavor to assist him in completing his Eagle Scout project and we are all grateful for his work. 

Click here or on the map below and a new window will open that will allow you to see the available trails across Groton that have been mapped in the new format.  To use the map, simply click on one of the placemarks.  A pop-up bubble will appear with information on the trail located there.  To view the map of the actual trail, click the link in the pop-up to zoom to the map.  Maps are interactive, so most icons are clickable and will return information on the location selected.  Maps can be printed as well.  Please take note of browser behavior for different browsers in the printing section below.

The default background view for the map can be changed as well by selecting the desired background from the drop-down menu at the top right of the screen.  The drop-down menu next to the map view drop-down has many additional options and tips that can be used to customize the map view.

Next click on the hiker icon denoting a mapped trail for the trail that most interests you.  A detailed trail map will then be visible for you. 

As we map additional trails in town we will add the appropriate maps and links for you to explore.

Browser settings

Internet Explorer 9  Most functions will work fine, but printing requires that the browser be set in Compatibility Mode to work as this time.  First time doing this, click Tools->Compatibility View Settings.  Add mappingsupport.com to the table of sites and Close.  Click Tools->Compatibility View to turn it on.  Once mappingsupport.com has been added to the list of sites, then subsequent visits just require that Compatibility View be turned on.

This setting change should not be necessary in Internet Explorer 8.

Chrome  No special settings needed at this time.

Firefox  No special settings needed at this time.

Printing

Internet Explorer 8 and 9  Generally, the trail will need to be shifted to the left side of the screen.  Then, click File->Print Preview to view the map.  Adjust as needed to set the trail fully on the map, change portrait to landscape, etc., then click Print.

Google Chrome No special seettings needed.

Firefox  Click File->Print Preview.  If map is formatted as desired, click Print.  Otherwise, go back and adjust, then print.

Looking to help

The Groton Trails Committee is always looking for help mapping trails.  Do you have a GPS unit (handheld, watch, etc.) and would you like to help?  Walk or run one of the many trails in Groton using your unit.  Mark waypoints (points of interest), take pictures, write down comments, etc.  Export the track and waypoint information from your GPS unit preferably in .gpx format, but .csv, .kmz, .tpo, and other formats can be used.

Send us your information, files, pictures, notes, etc. along with any other information you would like to provide.  If we have questions, we’ll contact you for more information.
